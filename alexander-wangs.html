The Origin of Alexander Jen Wang

My name Alexander finds its roots in ancient Greek, meaning "defender of the people"—a fitting moniker for someone drawn to building tools that help and protect others. The name Jen (振) comes from Chinese heritage, often meaning "to shake, excite, or invigorate," representing energy and forward momentum. Wang (王), one of the most common Chinese surnames, historically means "king" or "ruler," though in modern times it simply represents my family lineage. Together, Alexander Jen Wang embodies a bridge between cultures: the classical Western tradition of leadership and protection, combined with the Eastern values of energy and heritage.
